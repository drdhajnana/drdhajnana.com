 Directions for questions 01 to 04: Refer the table below and answer the following questions.

1. Following table gives some incomplete information on the shares purchased by four friends Amitabh, Rajesh,  Shashi and Vinod of four different companies A, B, C and D are

Additional Information:
(i) Amitabh's average shares is 72.5 in all the four companies
(ii) The average shares of company A for all four friends is 67.5.
(iii) Rajesh's Total shares for all the four companies is 1 : 6 times Shashi's in company A.
(iv) Rajesh and Vinod have the same number of shares in company D.
(v) The total share purchased from company B is 10 less than the total share purchased from company A for all four friends. 
(vi) The average of total number of shares purchased by all four friends is 262.5.
(vii) All shares are of the same costs.
Who had the least number of shares?

Options:
Option a: Amitabh
Option b: Rajesh 
Option c: Shashi 
Option d: Vinod

Answer:

Option c: Shashi

Lets complete the table first.
(i) Amitabh's average share is 72.5, So,
80 + 70 + x + 80 = avg 72.5
80 + 70 + x + 80 = 72.5 x 4 = 290
x = 290 - (80 + 70 + 80) = 60 => Company C's share.
(ii) company A's average share.
80 + 60 + x + 90 = avg 67.5
80 + 60 + x + 90 = 67.5 x 4
x = 270 - ( 80 + 60 + 90 )
x = 40 => Shashi's share in company A.
(iii) Rajesh's Total shares for all the four companies is 1 : 6 times Shashi's in company A.
Shashi's share in company A = 40.
Thus  Rajesh's Total shares = 240
(iv) Rajesh and Vinod have the same number of shares in company D.
Vinod has 60. So, Rajesh's share in Company D = 60
and Rajesh's share in Company B = 240 - (60 + 60 + 60) = 60
(vi) The average of total number of shares purchased by all four friends is 262.5.
Thus Vinod's total number of shares = 290 + 240 +220 + x = 262.5 x 4
=> x = 1050 - (290 + 240 + 220) = 300
Vinod's share in company B is
300 = 90 + x + 70 + 60.
x = 300 - (90 + 70 + 60)
x = 300 - 220 = 80
(v) The total share purchased from company B is 10 less than the total share purchased from company A for all four friends.
total share purchased from company A for all four friends = 80 + 60 + 40 + 90 = 270
Thus total share purchased from company B = 270 - 10 = 260
Shashi's share in company B = 260 - (70 + 60 + 80) = 50.
Thus Shashi's share in company D = 220 - (40 + 50 + 60) = 70.
This completes the table.

table below Shashi has the least number of shares.

Question No. 09: MCQ (with negative marking)

 There are two clocks. One of them loses 2 minutes in 12 hours and another one gains 6 minutes in 36 hours. Both are set right at 12 a.m. on January 1st 2000 (New Year). What is the next occurrence of both showing correct time

Options:
Option a: 12 a.m., March 31st 2000
Option b: 12 a.m., March 30th 2000
Option c: 12 noon, March 30th 2000
Option d: 12 noon, March 30th 2000

Answer:

Option a: 12 a.m., March 31st 2000
One gains 6 minutes in 36 hours. That means it gains 2 minutes every 12 hours.
Another loses 2 minutes in 12 hours, That is, difference = 4 minutes every 12 hours.
They will be on the same time when the difference between them is 12 hours.
1 minute = 12/4 = 3 hours
Therefore, 3 x 12 x 60 = 2160 or 90 days.
=> January 31 days + February 29 days + March 30 days = total 90 days.
=> 12 a.m., March 31st 2000

Question No. 14: MCQ (with negative marking)

 In ▲PQR, PQ = 53, and PR = 70. A circle with center P and radius PQ intersect QR at points R and X. Moreover RX and QX have integer lengths. What is QR? 

Options:
Option a: 11
Option b: 41
Option c: 61
Option d: 81

Answer:

Option b: 41
Let x represent RX, and let y represent QX. QR = x + y.
Since the circle goes through Q and X, PQ = PX = 53.
Stewart's theorem yields a relation between the side lengths and a cevian length of a triangle.
Now use Stewart's Theorem. 
xy(x + y) + 53² (x + y) = 70²y + 53²x
x²y + xy² +53²x + 53²y = 70²y + 53²x
x² + xy + 53² = 70²
(Since y cannot be equal to 0, dividing both sides of the equation by y is allowed.)
Or, x(x + y) = (70 + 53) (70 - 53)
Or, x(x + y) = 2091
The prime factors of 2091 are, 3 , 17, 41. Obviously, x < x + y. 
Therefore, x is 17 and x + y  is 41

Question No. 24: Not an MCQ (No negative marking)

 David and Rahim are friends whose income are in the ratio of 3 : 5 and their expenditures in the ratio of 5 : 7. Calculate the ratio of their savings, given that Rahim saves one fourth of his income.

Answer:

We take appropriate constants of proportionality in the two cases.
Income of David is 3x
Income of Rahim is 5x
Expenditure of David is 5y
Expenditure of Rahim is 7y
Savings of David = 3x - 5y
Savings of Rahim = 5x - 7y
We have been given that Rahim saves a fourth of his income.
5x - 7y = ¹/₄(5x)
=> 15x = 28y => x/y = 28/15
=> x = 28K and y = 15K
=> (3x - 5y)/(5x - 7y)
=> ((3 x 28) - (5 x 15))/((5 x 28) - (7 x 15))
=> 9k / 35k
=> Therefore, Ratio of their savings is 9 : 35
